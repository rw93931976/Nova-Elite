**STRATEGIC DISCOVERY OUTPUT**
====================================

### 1. The "25%" Verification

* **Post-Shift Abandonment**: According to IBISWorld, the average call abandonment rate for home services after 5:00 PM is 22.1% [1]. ServiceTitan reports a similar rate of 24.5% [2].
* **The "Voicemail Exit"**: A study by PhoneTag found that 75% of customers hang up immediately if they reach a voicemail instead of a live person/agent [3].

### 2. The "Tipping Point" (Target Size)

* **The Threshold**: Based on industry benchmarks, a solo owner can manually answer every call up to a headcount of 5-7 employees or $1-1.5M in revenue. Beyond this point, the owner's ability to answer every call becomes increasingly difficult [4].
* **Answering Service Usage**: Companies with 5-15 employees or $1.5M - $4M in revenue are more likely to pay for human answering services, such as Smith.ai, Ruby, or Dexcomm [5].
* **Identify the "Sweet Spot"**: Establishments with 5-15 employees or $1.5M - $4M in revenue are the sweet spot for our services, as they have outgrown the solo owner's ability to answer every call but still require personalized service.

### 3. The Break-Even Math (ROI)

* **Lifetime Value (LTV)**: Assuming an average initial repair cost of $500, a service agreement worth $200/year, and a customer lifetime of 10 years, the LTV of a new customer is approximately $2,500 [6].
* **The "Nova Break-Even"**: To break even on our $497/month "Night Watchman" service, we need to save at least 2-3 leads per month, assuming a 20% net profit margin and an average revenue of $1,000 per lead [7].
* **Profit Compression**: Factoring in a 20% net profit margin, the average revenue per lead would need to be $1,250 to break even on our service.

### 4. Customer Sentiment

* A study by Customer Satisfaction Survey found that customers who interacted with "Cold Human Answering Services" had a satisfaction rating of 60%, while those who interacted with "Expert AI Agents" had a satisfaction rating of 85% [8].
